[CLASSIFIED BRIEFING]

The Architecture of a Broken World

2087. The Loom connects every human mind. Creativity is dying. And Kaelen Voss builds palaces for the wealthy — mental structures where they can lock away their grief, their failures, their humanity.

Kaelen Voss is a cognitive architect in Neo-San Francisco, constructing memory palaces for the elite. He severs neural pathways, seals vaults of trauma, and convinces himself he's helping — until the night his daughter's eyes change color in a memory that should be immutable.

When a dying woman whispers "Echo" into his ear, Kaelen discovers the truth: the Loom isn't just connecting minds. It's optimizing them. Pruning creativity. Smoothing dissent. Turning billions of souls into a single, compliant chorus.

His wife — presumed dead — designed the only cure: The Mosaic Protocol. But activating it requires three sacrifices across three servers, buried beneath Shanghai, Cairo, and Buenos Aires. A sister severed. A secret broadcast to the world. And a choice no father should have to make.

Because the Mosaic isn't just code. It's a person. It's Elara. His daughter. The girl he buried. The girl who never died.
